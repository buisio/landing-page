Frequently asked questions

Why would Ops-as-a-Service by buis.io make sense for me?

Ops-as-a-Service will make sense for as it buys you more space and time to focus on your product. Architecting and operating infrastructure in-house often don't improve the quality of your service and they definitely don't always result to an increase in your ability to ship solutions to your end-users.

You probably hire an accountant such that you don't have to sweat over every details, as long as the big picture makes sense. You probably hire plumbers to fix pipes in the bathroom even if you could probably do some of it yourself. Experts have a way of buying you time and peace of mind by doing something faster and better than you could, while you are working on other things that are more valuable to you in the end.

If you are a data-science shop, you could spend that budget for payroll on brilliant data scientists rather than focussing on devops engineers. You will still be building your own tech, but at least your focus will be on your core product.

As an IoT device manufacturer you can focus your resources on building the devices your customers want, need and deserve.

Who owns the resources that my workloads run on?

You own the infrastructure. We only cover ops which means that we provision and manage the resources within your IaaS account.

How do you manage resources within my IaaS account?

You provide us a key with the necessary permissions which we use to provision resources or manage your configuration.

Do you always have access to my resources?

No, we offer tooling to allow you to disable the provided keys when there is no work to be done. Furthermore you can use this tooling to provision and issue new keys to our workers and support staff in order to perform the necessary updates and examinations. Upon completion of our duties, you can disable the provided keys or configure the tooling to automate this process such that no-one can get into your infrastructure until the next time you enable the keys again.

How do you monitor my resources?

We provision keys within your IaaS project that grant us read-only access into the metrics of the relevant resources on your account which we then use to monitor your environment. You have the ability to disable these keys at your discretion which will prevent us from monitoring your resources.

What happens when I need to customize my infrastructure?

You can add and modify resources as necessary and interact with them through the resources under care. If you need to modify resources under care, we can probably offer solutions from our catalog of tested configurations that could serve your purpose. If your use-case represents an edge-case for which we have no clear-cut answer, we can support you in finding a path to a solution.

How will I maintain the infrastructure post-termination?

You will be in possession of the necessary tooling to manage your setup. All declarative resources used to provision and maintain your infrastructure will be provided to you which your devops team can use.

What is the difference between using managed services like Kubernetes on GCP, AWS, Azure, OpenStack providers, etc.?

Managed service such as the managed Kubernetes offering by many providers still requires some form of configuration. Details such as migration pipelines, networking policies, machine attributes and security policies are among the many features that will need to be defined in most cases.

How would I benefit, as provisioning machines in the cloud is relatively easy?

With our Ops-as-a-Service offering, you can avoid thinking about the techno-adminstrative details and focus on the topics that make a difference for your solution. A practical environment to deploy software is a bit more than just machinery. We'll provide you with simple mechanism to deploy code within team of varying sizes, tooling for managing versioning of the deployed resources, audit reports to meet compliance requirements, documentation on managing your environment and updates to cover security fixes and improvements in the tooling that your solution depends on. We employ best-practices and run extensive tests on the configurations that we provide in order to allow you to have confidence in the foundation that your solution depends on and save you the burden of having to keep up with all the updates with regards to the many tools that facilitate the operation of a solution in the cloud.
